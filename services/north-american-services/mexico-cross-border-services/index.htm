Frequently Asked Questions

Does Werner service all gateways? 

Werner provides CTPAT-certified freight transportation and cross-docking services at major gateways between the U.S. and Mexico. 

Do you have dedicated capacity in Mexico? 

To handle capacity demand on both sides of the border, Werner has 2,000+ trailers in the Mexico market, including flatbed and heavy/specialized. We also maintain capacity through 270+ interchange agreements and our network of Mexico carriers. 

Can I get freight-tracking updates? 

Werner’s technology allows shippers to receive alerts via email with an estimated delivery time, notices of possible delays and notifications the cargo has arrived at the border. 

Is the staff bilingual? 

Werner provides bilingual support on both sides of the border to assist in translation. Our bilingual resources include agents, agency staff, and corporate sales and operations experts. 

What are the benefits of working with a Werner agent? 

Werner agents are independent business owners who serve as your dedicated cross-border shipping coordinator from start to finish. With their extensive industry knowledge, no transportation challenge is too big. Agents excel at creating custom solutions to fit the requirements of your freight while providing your shipment with the individualized attention it deserves. 

Does Werner have interchange agreements? 

Werner has 270+ interchange agreements with capacity providers in Mexico. We continually evaluate and add to this network to maintain the highest quality of service and safety for our customers. 

Does Werner have a presence in the U.S. and Mexico? 

Werner has a robust network of agents, owner-operators and third-party capacity providers across the U.S. and Mexico to provide full coverage service and support. Members of Werner’s Mexico Cross-Border Services team are also strategically positioned near key gateways, to provide guidance and coordination on both sides of the border.
