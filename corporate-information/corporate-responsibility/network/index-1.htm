Our Network & Our Communities

The secret behind Werner’s success is the network of independent business owners and corporate employees powering our unique business model. Werner is committed to supporting the people in our network and the communities in which we operate.

Diversity and Inclusion

At our core, Werner is about providing opportunity to people regardless of background. We do not tolerate racism or discriminatory behavior and strongly believe that diversity and inclusion make us stronger as a company.

Werner’s network includes a tremendously diverse group of backgrounds and viewpoints. We strive to maintain an empowering platform for employees, agents, BCOs and other third party capacity providers that is fair and inclusive of these different backgrounds and viewpoints.

Our business model is built on helping diverse individual entrepreneurs – regardless of race, gender or background – become successful in our network. Our focus as a company is on safely moving freight and supporting the entrepreneurs in our network to be successful. We support diversity and inclusion within our business and our network.

Working Against Human Trafficking

Werner is committed to working with industry groups and supply chain partners in the fight against human trafficking. We recognize that truckers are one of the first lines of defense to identify, report on and help prevent human trafficking. Orientation for all new BCOs at Werner includes information on human trafficking.

We are a corporate sponsor of Truckers Against Trafficking (TAT) and have worked with the organization to educate and certify over 3,650 of our BCOs and employees on the proper actions when encountering potential trafficked victims. Representatives of TAT also are regularly invited to BCO safety meetings and other Werner events.

Werner has signed the U.S. Department of Transportation’s Transportation Leaders Against Human Trafficking (TLAHT) pledge that calls on leaders in the transportation industry to commit to employee education, raising public awareness and measuring the collective impact to end human trafficking. In 2022 Werner pledged to continue its efforts to combat human trafficking by supporting the Florida Attorney General's 100% Club initiative. 

Werner also uses our own publications to bring attention to the fight against human trafficking. These are examples of articles that have appeared in Werner’s “Road to Success®” magazine, which has a print run of 100,000 copies and is distributed to all of our independent agents and BCOs as well as to over 600 truck stops throughout the USA. 

Our Employees

We believe our employees are among our most important resources and are critical to our continued success. Werner provides opportunity to qualified candidates for employment regardless of background. We do not tolerate discriminatory behavior and strongly believe that diverse perspectives and a collaborative culture lead to better business outcomes. The Company complies with all applicable federal and state laws pertaining to its employment practices and will ensure its policies are respected and implemented. This policy applies to many aspects of employment, including, but not limited to, hiring, pay, training, promotions, and all terms and conditions of employment and termination.

In accordance with the Americans with Disabilities Act, the Company will provide equal employment opportunities to qualified individuals with disabilities. Werner will make reasonable accommodations for the known physical or mental disabilities of an otherwise qualified applicant or employee, unless the proposed accommodation would create undue hardship. Employees and applicants requiring an accommodation in order to perform the essential functions of their job, should contact Human Resources to discuss a possible reasonable accommodation. The Company will consider any such request for accommodation, as well as other possible alternative accommodations that might allow employees to perform their jobs. The Company reserves the right to determine what, if any, reasonable accommodation it will implement.

In accordance with the Vietnam Era Veterans Readjustment Act of 1974, Werner will provide equal employment opportunities to those who are qualified disabled veterans and/or veterans of the Vietnam Era.

BCO All-Star Celebration & Appreciation Days

The annual Werner BCO All-Star Celebration honors an accomplished group of independent owner-operators for their commitment to safety and service excellence. Eligible Werner owner-operators are inducted into the newest class of Million Mile Safe Drivers, for reaching one, two, three, even four million miles without a preventable accident. Some are recognized as Roadstar® honorees, Werner’s highest honor for truck owner-operators.

Werner also holds appreciation events across the country exclusively for our network of independent owner-operators. These events include educational business sessions, expert guest speakers, safety education, panel discussions, food and prizes.

To reward our drivers and in show of appreciation, every year Werner gives away two new trucks. One goes to an eligible Million Mile Safe Driver or Roadstar honoree during the Werner All-Star Truck Giveaway. The other is given to one lucky owner-operator as part of the Deliver to Win Truck Giveaway

Agent Convention

The Werner Agent Convention is a celebration designed to congratulate Werner’s independent agents on their individual outstanding revenue performance. In addition to revenue awards, other honors presented at the annual event include the Mutual Understanding of Safety Together (M.U.S.T) Customer of the Year, Werner Safety Officer (LSO) of the Year, and Rookie of the Year awards. The multi-day event offers agents networking opportunities, valuable business insights sessions and fun activities in between.

Star of Quality Award

Each month, deserving independent business capacity owners (BCOs) and independent agents earn Star of Quality awards, recognizing efforts “above and beyond,” and individual dedication to service and their communities. Anyone can nominate an agent or BCO for the award, which ranges from Bronze to Platinum levels.

An Award Winning Culture

Werner has been recognized by Forbes magazine as one of America’s Best Midsize Employers for 2024 and is ranked 55th among the companies that made the list, achieving the highest ranking in the Transportation and Logistics category.

Werner System, Inc. was proud to receive the Responsible Suppliers Award by global chemistry leader The Chemours Company in the logistics category for quality, innovation and sustainability improvements across the Chemours’ supply chain in 2023.

Werner’s culture supports gender diversity and career advancement opportunities for women – and is why we were recognized in 2024 by the Women in Trucking Association (WIT) as one of the “Elite 30” top companies for women to work for in transportation. To be named, a company must foster gender diversity, provide competitive compensation and benefits, flexible hours and work requirements, professional development and career advancement opportunities.

Werner also has been recognized by the not-for-profit organization Women Executive Leadership, Inc. with an Elevate Award for advancing gender diversity in the boardroom. Currently, three of the eight members of Werner’s board of directors are women, including our Chairman of the Board, Diana M. Murphy.

BCO Benevolence Fund

Since the Werner BCO Benevolence Fund was created in 2005, it has provided financial assistance to hundreds of BCOs in hardship situations. The fund was established to distribute financial assistance to qualified BCOs in emergency situations who do not have the assets to meet their existing financial obligations and/or immediate basic living expenses for their families. Werner BCOs and truck operators employed by Werner BCOs, and their immediate family members, are eligible to receive assistance from the fund. Contributions to the Benevolence Fund are collected throughout the year at Werner’s various events. To find out if you qualify, to apply or to assist with Werner’s Benevolence Fund email BCOfund@werner.com or call 800-872-9541.

Werner Scholarship Program

The Werner Scholarship Fund was established in 1995 to recognize and reward outstanding undergraduate academic achievement and civic involvement of children of Werner employees, independent business capacity owners (BCOs) leased to Werner and independent sales agents. Werner Scholarship Fund recipients are well-rounded individuals who succeed both in and out of the classroom and whose qualities exemplify Werner’s commitment to excellence. Professional Associations

Werner is a corporate member of the Florida Trucking Association and the Illinois Trucking Association whose purposes are to promote and protect the interests of the trucking industry.

In addition to these statewide associations, Werner is involved in industry-specific organizations such as the American Transportation Research Institute (ATRI), National Defense Transportation Association (NDTA), the Specialized Carriers & Rigging Association (SC&RA), the Transportation Intermediaries Association (TIA), and the Transportation Marketing and Sales Association (TMSA).

Werner is also a corporate member of the Women in Trucking Association (WIT) and Jacksonville Women’s Leadership Forum, both of which are focused on bridging the gender diversity gap in the workplace. 

Philanthropy & Volunteerism

Werner is committed to supporting the independent business owners and corporate employees in our unique network, along with the communities in which we all operate.

For more than a decade, Werner has supported The Women’s Board of Wolfson Children’s Hospital in Jacksonville, Florida. The mission of the hospital is to care for each and every child that comes through its doors and, by offering its support, Werner hopes to contribute to the health of the next generation.

Similarly, Werner sponsors and supports:

  • American Cancer Society’s Relay for Life
  • Feeding Northeast Florida
  • School Supply Drives Jacksonville and Rockford
  • The United Way of Northeast Florida
  • The United Way of Rock River Valley, Illinois

Additionally, Werner’s corporate employees aim to brighten the holidays by collecting bike donations and hosting a toy drive in an annual Angel Tree gift program. This program directly supports the Child Guidance Center in Jacksonville, Florida, which provides behavioral solutions to local children and their families.

Werner employees who work out of the Rockford, Illinois office, participate in an Angel Tree program to benefit the Winnebago/Boone Foster Care Alliance. This organization is the collaborative result of various agencies in both Winnebago and Boone Counties, and its Adopt-An-Angel program provides holiday gifts to local foster children and children involved with child welfare services.

Many entrepreneurs within Werner’s network also offer exemplary service to their communities. Werner is proud to be affiliated with independent agents and BCOs who make a positive impact on their own local communities.
