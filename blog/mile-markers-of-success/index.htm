Mile Markers of Success

October 17, 2018
  • Share via Facebook
  • Share via LinkedIn
  • Share via email

Werner System celebrated its 30th anniversary in October 2018. After three decades in business, a glance in the rear view mirror reveals Werner has passed many significant “mile markers” along the company’s steady course as an industry leader on the road to success.

  1. Werner was formed in 1988.
  2. Werner has held a Safety Thursday Conference Call every month since 1991 – that’s 27 years!
  3. Werner became a publically traded company – listed on NASDAQ in 1993.
  4. Werner created the term business capacity owner (BCO) to describe the company’s independent truck owner-operators.
  5. Werner began presenting the Roadstar® honor to BCOs in 1990. Since then, 469 men and women have earned Roadstar status.
  6. Werner’s Star of Quality Award program, which honors those who go above and beyond, started in 1993. More than 8,000 stars have been awarded to Werner agents and BCOs.
  7. Since the Werner Scholarship Fund was created in 1995, 334 students have received a share of $802,000 in funds from Werner.
  8. Werner has given away 38 semi-trucks to business capacity owners since 1994.
  9. In 1997, Werner moved its headquarters to Jacksonville, Florida.
  10. In 1998, Werner began requiring all owner-operators to become hazmat certified.
  11. Werner’s award-winning The Road to Success® magazine was first published in 1999.
  12. The “swoosh” around the star in Werner’s logo represents the road to success.
  13. Werner introduced the first cellphone accessible load board in the industry in 2000.
  14. The first celebration honoring Million Mile Safe Drivers was a banquet held in 2001.
  15. There are currently 1,060 active Million Mile Safe Drivers at Werner – that includes two, three and four million mile safe drivers.
  16. In 2003, Werner celebrated the first independent Werner agent to exceed $100 million in revenue.
  17. The first Werner BCO to achieve 4 million safe miles was Jerry Travis. He reached this milestone in 2004.
  18. The BCO Benevolence Fund was founded by Werner in 2005 to distribute financial assistance to qualified BCOs.
  19. Since its creation, the Werner BCO Benevolence Fund has provided financial assistance to 388 BCOs.
  20. Werner Canada was formed in 2007.
  21. More than 55 percent of Werner’s employees have been with the company for 10+ years.
  22. Werner has many longtime employees. In fact, the record of 51 years (and counting) is held by Nancy Brown.
  23. Through the military’s Training with the Industry program, each year a captain with the U.S. Army spends 12 months shadowing Werner’s government services team to learn about commercial logistics.
  24. Werner created and launched the Werner Maximizer® mobile app in 2017 to help BCOs find multileg runs.
  25. In 2017, Pricing Tools and Agent Analytics were created and designed to help independent agents grow their Werner agencies.
  26. 542 independent Werner agents reached $1+ million in revenue in 2017.*
  27. Werner opened its state-of-the-art cross-border logistics center in Laredo, Texas, in 2017.
  28. Werner hauled more than 1,903,000 loads in 2017.*
  29. 2018 - More than 10,000 trucks are provided by BCOs.*
  30. Werner is a $3.6 billion company – and growing!*

*Information provided is based on Werner's 2017 Form 10K.
